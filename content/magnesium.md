# Magnesium

## Definition

Magnesium is the "balancing" member of the big three. It is the third most
abundant ion in seawater and it keeps calcium and carbonate (alkalinity) in
solution so they don't precipitate out. Get magnesium right and calcium and
alkalinity become far easier to hold steady.

## Measurement & Units

Measured in **parts per million (ppm)** with a titration test kit. Magnesium is
consumed slowly, so **every week or two** is plenty once the tank is established
— but always check it when calcium and alkalinity stubbornly refuse to stay up,
because low magnesium is the usual culprit.

## Ideal Range

**1250–1350 ppm**, with natural seawater around **1280–1300 ppm**. A value near
1300 gives calcium and alkalinity the headroom they need without any downside.

## When It's Too High

Moderately high magnesium is generally well tolerated and rarely causes visible
harm. Very high levels (well above ~1500 ppm), usually from overdosing, can
stress some invertebrates and throw the ionic balance off; there's no benefit to
pushing it high.

## When It's Too Low

This is the important one: when magnesium is low, calcium and carbonate
**precipitate out of solution**, so calcium and alkalinity fall no matter how
much you dose. Coral growth stalls and coralline algae fade. Chronically low
magnesium is the hidden cause behind many "I can't keep my alkalinity up"
problems.

## Signs & Symptoms

- Calcium and/or alkalinity that keep dropping despite regular dosing.
- Coralline algae failing to spread; sluggish coral growth.
- Water changes barely move the numbers because the balance is off.

## Suggested Remedies

- **To raise:** dose a magnesium supplement (a magnesium chloride / sulfate
  blend). Correct low magnesium *before* trying to fix calcium and alkalinity.
- **To lower:** rarely needed — a water change with correctly-mixed salt brings
  an over-high value down gently.
- Raise magnesium gradually (a few tens of ppm per day) and re-test; once it's in
  range, calcium and alkalinity typically become much easier to stabilize.
