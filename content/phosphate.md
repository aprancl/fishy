# Phosphate

## Definition

Phosphate (PO₄) is a dissolved nutrient that enters the tank through food, fish
waste, and decaying matter. A trace of phosphate is normal and even necessary —
corals need some nutrients — but excess phosphate fuels nuisance algae and
interferes with coral calcification. It is managed as one half of the
nutrient pair with nitrate.

## Measurement & Units

Measured in **parts per million (ppm), i.e. mg/L**, with a colorimetric test kit;
a low-range or digital (Hanna-style) checker is best because reef targets are so
small. Test **weekly**, and interpret phosphate alongside nitrate — both near
zero can be as troublesome as both too high.

## Ideal Range

**0.03–0.10 ppm** for a mixed reef. Many SPS-focused keepers aim for the lower
end (~0.03 ppm); softies and LPS are comfortable a bit higher. The goal is a low
but **non-zero** level — stripping phosphate to a true zero can starve corals and
trigger a nutrient crash.

## When It's Too High

Elevated phosphate is the classic driver of **nuisance algae** — hair algae,
cyanobacteria, and dinoflagellate outbreaks. It also directly **inhibits coral
skeletal growth**, so SPS in particular grow slowly and lose color even when
alkalinity and calcium are perfect.

## When It's Too Low

Bottoming phosphate out (undetectable) removes a nutrient corals actually need.
It can cause **pale or bleached corals**, browning-out from stress, and often
coincides with dinoflagellate blooms that thrive in nutrient-starved water.

## Signs & Symptoms

- Green hair algae, cyano, or a brown/slimy film advancing on rock and glass.
- SPS corals dull, brown, or growing slowly despite good alkalinity.
- Corals pale or "washed out" when phosphate has been driven too low.

## Suggested Remedies

- **To lower:** improve export — better skimming, regular water changes, cutting
  back feeding, and running **GFO (granular ferric oxide)** or a phosphate-
  binding media. Bring it down gradually to avoid shocking corals.
- **To raise:** feed a little more, ease off phosphate media, or dose a
  phosphate/nutrient supplement to lift it off zero.
- Keep phosphate and **nitrate in balance** — chase steady, low-but-present
  nutrients rather than an absolute zero.
