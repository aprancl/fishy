# Calcium

## Definition

Calcium is one of the primary ingredients corals, clams, and coralline algae use
to build their calcium-carbonate skeletons and shells. Along with alkalinity and
magnesium it forms the "big three" that a reef keeper balances to support steady
growth.

## Measurement & Units

Measured in **parts per million (ppm), i.e. mg/L**, with a titration test kit.
Calcium moves more slowly than alkalinity, so testing **once or twice a week** is
usually enough once the tank is stable. Test calcium and alkalinity together —
they are consumed in tandem and are easiest to interpret side by side.

## Ideal Range

**400–450 ppm** for a growing reef, with natural seawater around **420 ppm**.
Values a little above or below are fine as long as they are stable and alkalinity
and magnesium are also in range.

## When It's Too High

Very high calcium (above ~500 ppm) makes it hard to keep alkalinity up at the
same time, because the two can **precipitate out** together — clouding the water,
coating heaters and pumps, and actually *lowering* alkalinity. Extremely high
calcium offers no benefit to corals.

## When It's Too Low

Low calcium starves the calcification process: coral and coralline growth slows,
new coral tips look thin or fail to harden, and clams struggle to extend their
shells. Low calcium usually appears alongside falling alkalinity when a fast-
growing tank isn't being dosed enough.

## Signs & Symptoms

- Slow or stalled coral growth; coralline algae fading rather than spreading.
- Difficulty holding alkalinity and calcium up at the same time (often too high).
- Cloudy water or scale on equipment after aggressive dosing (precipitation).

## Suggested Remedies

- **To raise:** dose a calcium supplement (calcium chloride) or run a two-part /
  balling routine that lifts calcium and alkalinity together; a calcium reactor
  or Kalkwasser suits high-demand tanks.
- **To lower:** it rarely needs active lowering — a water change with correctly-
  mixed salt brings an over-high value back toward natural levels.
- Keep **magnesium in range (~1300 ppm)** first; without enough magnesium,
  calcium and alkalinity won't stay up no matter how much you dose.
