# Salinity

## Definition

Salinity is the total dissolved salt content of the water — the single most
fundamental measure of a saltwater tank. It sets the osmotic environment every
fish, invertebrate, and coral lives in, so keeping it stable matters as much as
hitting the right number.

## Measurement & Units

Two common expressions of the same thing:

- **Specific gravity (SG)** — the density of the water relative to pure water,
  read on a hydrometer or refractometer. Natural seawater is about **1.025–1.026**
  at 25 °C.
- **Parts per thousand (ppt)** — grams of salt per kilogram of water; natural
  seawater is about **35 ppt**.

A **refractometer** (calibrated with RO/DI water or a calibration fluid) is far
more accurate than a swing-arm hydrometer. Because SG depends on temperature,
measure at a consistent tank temperature.

## Ideal Range

**1.024–1.026 SG (~33–35 ppt)** for a mixed reef. Reef keepers generally aim for
the natural-seawater end (1.025–1.026); fish-only systems tolerate a little
lower. Stability is more important than chasing a precise decimal.

## When It's Too High

Excess salinity dehydrates livestock — water is osmotically pulled out of their
tissues. Fish become stressed and lethargic, and corals may retract their polyps
and lose color. High salinity often creeps up from **evaporation** when top-off
water isn't keeping pace.

## When It's Too Low

Low salinity swells cells and stresses osmoregulation. Corals fail to extend and
can bleach; sensitive invertebrates (snails, shrimp, urchins) suffer or die.
Sudden drops — from a large freshwater top-off or a heavy dose of RO/DI — are
especially dangerous.

## Signs & Symptoms

- Corals not extending polyps, dull or fading color.
- Fish clamped, breathing hard, or hiding.
- Invertebrates sluggish or failing to right themselves.
- A steady day-over-day climb usually means evaporation outpacing top-off.

## Suggested Remedies

- **To lower:** slowly add RO/DI freshwater (never salt-free saltwater) over
  hours, or do a small water change with correctly-mixed lower-salinity water.
- **To raise:** mix salt into a batch of RO/DI to the target SG and add it
  gradually.
- Change salinity **slowly** — no more than ~0.001 SG per hour for sensitive
  corals and inverts.
- Automate evaporation replacement with an **auto top-off (ATO)** using RO/DI to
  hold salinity steady between water changes.
